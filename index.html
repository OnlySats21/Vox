Why Vox exists

They took the town square.
We're taking it back.

Social media was supposed to be ours. A place where real people spoke freely, found their people, and actually heard each other. Instead it became a machine — engineered to keep you angry, addicted, and profitable. Your feed isn't yours. It belongs to whoever paid the most to put it there.

Vox is Latin for voice. And that's exactly what got stolen. Not by accident — by design. Algorithms buried what mattered. Ads drowned out what was real. The people who built these platforms stopped asking "what do users want?" and started asking "what keeps them scrolling?"

We're done with that. Vox is built by people who were fed up — for people who are fed up. No ads. No algorithm deciding your reality. No billionaire's mood changing the rules overnight. Just your voice, the people you choose to follow, and a feed that respects your time. This is our platform. Let's build it together.
